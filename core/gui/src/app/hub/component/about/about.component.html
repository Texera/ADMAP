Alzheimer's Disease Multimodal Atlasing Project


The Alzheimer's Disease Multimodal Atlasing Project (ADMAP) is a collaborative initiative at the UCI Center for Neural Circuit Mapping.
It aims to advance research on Alzheimer's disease by providing a platform for researchers to upload, explore, and download datasets.

Key Features

  • Comprehensively characterize brain circuits susceptible to Alzheimer's disease type pathology and/or neurodegeneration in mouse models of Alzheimer's disease (AD).
  • Share data on the cellular connectome in the aging and AD brain in a common reference brain cell atlas that integrates both molecular and anatomical annotations.
  • Complement and extend research on vulnerable cell types to include the mapping of connectivity changes between cells in aging and AD and provide a greater understanding of the mechanisms underlying resilience and vulnerability in AD.
